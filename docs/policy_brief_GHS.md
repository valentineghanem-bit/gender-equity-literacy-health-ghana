# Policy Brief — Closing Ghana's North–South Child-Health Gap: Invest Upstream in Girls' Education and Gender Equity

**For:** Ghana Health Service · Ministry of Health · Ministry of Education · Ministry of Gender, Children & Social Protection
**From:** Valentine Golden Ghanem · **Date:** June 2026 · **Evidence base:** 2022 Ghana DHS (16 regions) + 2021 Census (261 districts)
*Ecological, associational analysis — see limitations.*

## The problem in one line
Children are dying younger, are more anaemic, and are more often born to adolescent mothers in the **same northern districts** where fewer girls complete school and fewer women have decision-making power — and these disadvantages cluster together geographically.

## What the data show
- **A stark north–south divide.** Northern belt vs Coastal/South: under-five mortality **49 vs 40** per 1,000; child anaemia **66% vs 45%**; adolescent birth rate **87 vs 55** per 1,000; women's secondary education **41% vs 77%**; female illiteracy **48% vs 22%**.
- **Disadvantage is spatially concentrated, not scattered.** Female illiteracy and poverty are strongly clustered (statistically significant hotspots) across the northern savannah — robust whether measured at region or district level.
- **The gap is upstream, not in the clinics.** Reproductive-health-service coverage (antenatal care, skilled delivery) is **already high (~89%) nationwide**. Our analysis finds that the link between women's education/equity and child health does **not** run through service use — so expanding services further is unlikely to close the gap.

## What this means for policy
The efficient lever is **upstream and place-based**: girls' education and gender equity, concentrated where deprivation clusters.

## Recommendations
1. **Geographically target** girls'-education and gender-equity programmes to the northern high-illiteracy cluster (North East, Northern, Savannah, Upper East, Upper West) — use the district maps in this study for micro-targeting.
2. **Keep girls in school** (secondary completion, re-entry after pregnancy) and **expand adult/female literacy** in the cluster.
3. **Pair with gender-equity action** — decision-making, asset ownership, and reduction of intimate-partner violence (the three SWPER dimensions).
4. **Maintain, don't over-invest in, RH-service expansion** where coverage is already high; shift marginal resources upstream.
5. **Cross-sectoral delivery** (Health + Education + Gender) with shared district targets and a simple equity dashboard.

## SDG alignment
SDG 3 (child survival, target 3.2) · SDG 4 (education) · SDG 5 (gender equality).

## Limitations (for fair use)
Findings are **ecological and cross-sectional** (area-level associations, not individual causal effects); regional inference rests on 16 units. Use for **prioritisation and targeting**, not for attributing individual outcomes. Full methods, code and data: the project GitHub repository.
